The Tradition of ‘Shagun’

By Pinterest

In India, giving cash as a gift, known as ‘Shagun,’ is a cherished tradition symbolizing good luck and blessings for newlyweds. This practice often involves presenting cash in auspicious amounts ending in 1, such as ₹101 or ₹1101, reflecting the giver’s heartfelt intentions for the couple’s prosperous future.

Practicality &

Flexibility

Indian weddings are lavish, making gift selection challenging. Enter cash gifts, which provide unmatched flexibility for the couple. They can use the money for their dream honeymoon, invest in a new home, or purchase something special. In today’s practical world, cash gifts are a thoughtful and empowering choice for newlyweds.

Cultural Sensitivity & Acceptance

Recognizing cultural nuances is essential. In Indian culture, cash gifts at weddings are not only acceptable but highly valued. They symbolize traditional blessings shared across generations. Accepting cash gifts with grace and gratitude is integral to Indian wedding customs, making them a perfectly appropriate choice for celebrating the couple’s union.

The Power of

Choice

Ultimately, gifting is about making the recipient feel valued. While some believe physical gifts demonstrate more thought, others appreciate the practicality and freedom of cash gifts. It’s essential to consider the couple’s preferences. If you know them well, assess whether they’d prefer a tangible gift or the flexibility of cash.

Wrapping It Up

with Love

Whether you choose to give a beautifully crafted gift or an envelope filled with blessings in the form of cash, remember that it’s the intention and love behind the gift that truly matters. Indian weddings celebrate the union of hearts, and your gift, be it cash or otherwise, is a token of your participation in their joyous journey.

So, lovely people, don’t fret over the appropriateness of giving cash at an Indian wedding. Embrace the tradition, or give it your unique twist, and let your gift reflect your warm wishes for the couple’s happily ever after.
