What Factors Affect
The Cost Of Wedding
Catering?

Food is an integral part of Indian weddings, and choosing the right catering service is crucial to ensure that your guests have a memorable dining experience. So, what factors affect the cost of wedding catering? Understanding the cost of wedding catering can be a bit tricky as it depends on various factors such as the type of cuisine, the number of guests, the venue, and the level of customization required.

Types Of Cuisine

By Pinterest
By Pinterest

The type of cuisine you choose for your wedding plays a significant role in determining the cost of catering.

Tradational
Indian Cuisine

Indian weddings are known for their diverse and rich food culture. The cost of traditional Indian catering can vary depending on the region and the specific dishes chosen. For example, North Indian cuisine may include dishes like Butter Chicken, Biryani, and Naan, while South Indian cuisine may feature Dosa, Idli, and Sambar.

Specialty
Cuisine

If you opt for specialty cuisines such as Mughlai, Rajasthani, Gujarati, or Coastal, the cost may be slightly higher due to the unique ingredients and cooking techniques involved.

Menu Selection

The selection of dishes and the number of courses in the menu can significantly impact the cost of wedding catering.

Vegetarian vs.
Non-Vegetarian

Vegetarian menus are generally more affordable compared to non-vegetarian menus, as the cost of ingredients like meat and seafood can be higher. However, both options can be customized to suit your budget and preferences.

Number Of
Courses

A multi-course meal with appetizers, main course, desserts, and beverages will be more expensive compared to a simple buffet or plated meal with fewer courses.

The number of guests attending your wedding is a crucial factor that affects the cost of catering.

Economies
of Scale

The cost per plate will decrease as the number of guests increases due to economies of scale. However, it is essential to ensure that the quality of food and service is not compromised, irrespective of the number of guests.

Level of Service

The level of service you choose for your wedding catering can also
influence the cost.

Buffet vs.
Plated Meal

Buffet service is generally more affordable compared to a plated meal, as it requires fewer servers. However, a plated meal offers a more formal and elegant dining experience.

Live Counters &
Food Stations

Live counters for dishes like chaat, pasta, dosa, or grills can add an interactive and fun element to your wedding but may increase the overall cost.

Venue Restrictions And
Additional Charges

By Pinterest

Some venues may have restrictions or additional charges for outside caterers, kitchen facilities, or corkage fees for alcohol, which can impact the overall cost of catering.
