How Much Should I Spend

on Wedding Gifts

for A Wedding?

Hey there, fellow wedding guests and gift-givers! So, you’ve received that elegant invitation to a friend or family member’s wedding, and now you’re pondering over the perennial question: “How much should I spend on a wedding gift?” It’s a dilemma we’ve all faced at some point, but fret not! In this blog post, we’re going to unravel this mystery and offer some friendly guidance tailored to the Indian context. So, grab a chai, get comfy, and let’s dive in!

First off, let’s establish one thing – there’s no one-size-fits-all answer here. The amount you should spend on a wedding gift can vary based on various factors like your relationship with the couple, your budget, and cultural norms. But fear not, we’re here to break it down and make this decision-making process a breeze, desi style!

Your Relationship

with the Couple

One of the most cherished gifts you can give an Indian couple is a stunning saree for the bride and a dashing sherwani for the groom. These traditional garments hold immense cultural significance and are perfect for special occasions and celebrations. Opt for rich fabrics like silk or chiffon, adorned with intricate embroidery or embellishments. Not only will the couple appreciate the thoughtfulness of this gift, but they’ll also look absolutely radiant on their big day!

Setting a

Budget

Before you embark on your gift-hunting expedition, it’s crucial to set a budget for yourself. Take a moment to assess how much you’re comfortable spending and stick to that budget. Remember, it’s not about the size of the gift, but the thought and love behind it that truly matter. Don’t feel pressured to break the bank – a heartfelt gift within your means will be cherished by the couple.

Consider the

Cost of Living

Keep in mind the cost of living and prevailing economic conditions when deciding on your gift budget. In India, the cost of wedding gifts can vary greatly depending on factors like the city, socio-economic background, and cultural expectations. For instance, if you’re attending a wedding in a metro city like Mumbai or Delhi, you might need to budget more compared to a wedding in a smaller town or rural area.

Cultural Norms and

Expectations

By Pinterest

Cultural norms and expectations play a significant role in determining gift-giving etiquette at Indian weddings. In many Indian cultures, it’s customary to give cash or checks as gifts, commonly referred to as “shagun” or “blessings” for the couple’s future. The amount you give can vary depending on factors like your relationship with the couple, regional customs, and personal preferences. It’s always a good idea to seek guidance from family members or friends if you’re unsure.

Think About the Couple’s

Needs and Lifestyle

When selecting a wedding gift, consider the couple’s needs, preferences, and lifestyle. Do they have a wedding registry? If so, peruse their wishlist and choose something that aligns with their tastes. If they haven’t registered or you prefer a more personalized approach, think about their hobbies, interests, and future plans as a couple. Practical gifts or experiences tailored to their lifestyle can be incredibly thoughtful and appreciated.

It’s the Thought

That Counts

At the end of the day, remember that it’s the thought and sentiment behind the gift that truly matter. Whether you opt for a lavish gift, a modest token, or something in between, what’s important is that it comes from the heart. A heartfelt note or card expressing your love and blessings can be just as meaningful as a pricey present.

How much you should spend on a wedding gift in India depends on various factors like your relationship with the couple, your budget, cultural norms, and the couple’s preferences. Set a budget, consider the cost of living, cultural expectations, and the couple’s needs, and remember that the most cherished gifts are those given with love and sincerity. So go ahead, choose a gift that reflects your warmth and blessings, and celebrate the joyous occasion with the happy couple!
